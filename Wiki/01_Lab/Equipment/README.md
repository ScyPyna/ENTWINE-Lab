# 🧰 Equipment
This section describes the standard operation procedures for our laboratory equipment. 

---
---
## 💡 Laser 
### 🔵 473 nm DPSS Laser System
The laboratory is equipped with a Laserglow LRS-0473 diode-pumped solid-state (DPSS) laser system operating at a nominal wavelength of 473 nm (blue region of the visible spectrum).

This laser provides a stable and well-defined beam with high spatial quality and narrow spectral linewidth. The system includes a laser head and a dedicated driver/power supply, with TTL and analog modulation capabilities depending on the configuration.

In our experimental setup, the 473 nm beam is primarily used for controlled optical stimulation and excitation of microalgae.

### ⚠️ Safety Note
This system falls within Class IIIb / IV safety classification depending on output configuration.
Proper laser safety procedures must always be followed, including the use of wavelength-appropriate protective eyewear and activation of interlocks when required.

## 🌡️ Incubator 

The incubator (Binder KB PRO Light Module, two-shelf configuration) is used to provide controlled temperature conditions for the growth and maintenance of algal cultures. Stable thermal environments are essential for reproducible physiological activity, cell division, and metabolic performance. Cultures are placed inside to ensure consistent incubation during experimental preparation, propagation, or storage phases, minimizing external environmental fluctuations.

---

## 🔥 Autoclave

The autoclave is a steam-based sterilization system used to eliminate microorganisms, spores, and biological contaminants from laboratory materials. It operates by exposing items to pressurized saturated steam at high temperature, ensuring effective sterilization of media, glassware, and waste.
In our laboratory, the autoclave is mainly used sterilizing liquid media (e.g., TAP before phosphate addition)
and glassware and reusable instruments (almost on a daily basis).
The effectiveness of sterilization depends on proper loading, correct cycle selection, and sufficient exposure time. Materials must be arranged to allow steam circulation, and bottles should never be sealed tightly during sterilization.

---

## 🧫 Laminar Flow Hood

The laminar flow hood provides a sterile working environment by directing filtered air in a unidirectional (laminar) flow across the workspace. This controlled airflow minimizes the risk of airborne contamination during sensitive procedures.
In our laboratory, the laminar flow hood is primarily used for the inoculation and propagation of algal cultures, the preparation of sterile media components and the handling of sterile consumables (as post-autoclave manipulations).
To maintain sterility, the hood should be turned on in advance to stabilize airflow, and the working surface must be disinfected before and after use.
